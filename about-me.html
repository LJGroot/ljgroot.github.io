About Me

Although I'm not a mathematician, I’ve always had a fascination with numbers. As a toddler, I was already preoccupied with data, rigorously counting and calculating how many motorcycles I saw on the road, even before I had ever been to school. Given the Garmin ever around my wrist and the elaborate spreadsheets I maintain to track my rock climbing progression, it’s safe to say my love for data hasn’t faded one bit.

After obtaining my Bachelor's degree from the College of Child Development and Education, and a Research Master's degree from the Graduate School of Child Development and Education, I pursued a doctoral degree at the Research Institute of Child Development and Education, as a member of the Methods and Statistics research group. Currently, I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Amsterdam School for Communication Research, where I study online media consumption as a part of the Macroscope project.
